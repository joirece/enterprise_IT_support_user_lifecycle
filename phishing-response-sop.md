# Phishing & Suspicious Email Response SOP

## Purpose
This procedure outlines the steps for identifying, reporting, and responding to phishing or suspicious email activity to protect organizational systems and data.

## Scope
Applies to all employees and IT support staff handling potential phishing or security-related email incidents.

---

## 1. Incident Identification
- [ ] User reports suspicious email (via ticket, email, or phone)
- [ ] Identify common phishing indicators:
  - Unexpected attachments or links
  - Urgent or threatening language
  - Unknown sender or spoofed email address
  - Requests for credentials or sensitive information

---

## 2. Initial Response
- [ ] Instruct user NOT to click links or download attachments
- [ ] Advise user to avoid replying to the email
- [ ] Request a screenshot or copy of the email if needed
- [ ] Ensure the email is preserved for analysis

---

## 3. Verification & Analysis
- [ ] Review sender email address and domain
- [ ] Inspect links (hover to verify actual URL)
- [ ] Check for known phishing patterns
- [ ] Determine if the email is malicious or safe

---

## 4. Containment Actions
- [ ] If malicious:
  - Instruct user to delete the email
  - Block sender/domain if applicable
  - Remove similar emails from other users (if system allows)
- [ ] If link was clicked:
  - Reset user password immediately
  - Revoke active sessions
  - Enforce MFA reset if necessary

---

## 5. Escalation
- [ ] Escalate confirmed phishing incidents to security team
- [ ] Report incident according to company policy
- [ ] Document severity and potential impact

---

## 6. User Awareness
- [ ] Educate user on phishing indicators
- [ ] Provide guidance on safe email practices
- [ ] Encourage reporting of future suspicious activity

---

## 7. Documentation & Ticket Closure
- [ ] Record incident details in ticketing system
- [ ] Document actions taken
- [ ] Attach evidence (screenshots, email headers if applicable)
- [ ] Mark ticket as resolved or escalated

---

## Security Notes
- Phishing is one of the most common attack vectors
- Early reporting significantly reduces risk
- Always prioritize containment and user safety
- Follow organizational incident response policies
